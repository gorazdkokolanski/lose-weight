Choose where your service provider is located

POPUP 1: Five-Eyes - the core group (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand) with the tightest data-sharing pact. Five Eyes countries have extensive surveillance programs, making VPNs based there (e.g., PIA) less ideal for privacy, that’s why choosing one outside their reach is critical.
POPUP 2: Fourteen-Eyes - refers to a group of 14 countries that share intelligence data. (US, UK, CA, AU, NZ, DE, FR, NL, IT, ES, SE, BE, DK, NO) cooperate to monitor communications, which impact privacy. For VPN users, a provider based in a Fourteen Eyes country may face legal pressure to share user data unless that employe safeguard measures.
POPUP 3: Decentralized - doesn’t use central servers owned by one company. Instead, it routes your internet traffic through a network of (other users’ computers) around the world. Think of it like borrowing Wi-Fi from many people instead of one provider. This makes it harder for governments or hackers to get a hold of your user logs, as there’s no single point to target.
